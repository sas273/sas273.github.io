Grade 7 • The Taiping Rebellion

Term 3 (Asia) • Week 24 • Day 5 • Estimated time: ~60 minutes

Overview Memory Task Spiral Review Nonfiction Reading Fiction Reading Applied Activity Wrap-Up Discovery & Enrichment

1) Lesson Overview

Objectives: Students will (a) recall five key facts about the Taiping Rebellion, (b) summarize how art reflects social change using Ren Xiong as an example, and (c) compare/contrast Taiping with a prior-learned rebellion using evidence.

2) Memory Task ~5–7 min

Today’s Facts: 5 key facts about the Taiping Rebellion

3) Spiral Review ~10 min

Mixed retrieval practice of prior rebellions and key terms.

4) Reading — Nonfiction ~10–12 min

Title: The Taiping Rebellion: Crisis and Change in Qing China

5) Reading — Fiction/Excerpt ~10–12 min

The city gates had a new name. Smoke lifted from cooking fires in alleys where soldiers now slept. Mei traced the carved character for “Heaven” with her finger on a wooden sign, wondering how long names could change before a city itself felt different.

6) Applied Activity — Compare & Contrast ~15–20 min

Venn diagram comparing Taiping with another rebellion. Use provided statements.

7) Wrap-Up ~5 min

Exit Ticket: (a) Name the Taiping leader. (b) State one Taiping goal. (c) Identify the city they made their capital.

8) Discovery & Enrichment (Optional)

Mini-research tasks, classroom debates, cross-media picks.
