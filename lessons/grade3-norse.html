Grade 3 • Norse Navigation

Term 1 (Europe) • Week 3 • Day 2 • Estimated time: ~60 minutes

Overview Memory Task Spiral Review Fiction Reading Nonfiction Reading Applied Activity Wrap-Up Discovery & Enrichment

1) Lesson Overview

Objectives: Students will (a) recall today’s multiplication facts, (b) describe two ways Norse sailors used nature for navigation, and (c) record and interpret simple data in a table.

  • Focus Topic: Norse sailors and nature-based navigation.
  • Cross-links: Math (facts & data), Science (animal behavior, stars), ELA (fiction & nonfiction comprehension).

2) Memory Task ~5–7 min

Today’s Facts: 2×2, 2×3, 2×4, 2×5

Quick Recall (Yesterday’s review)
  1. What is 2×1?
  2. What is 2×2?
  3. What is 2×3?

3) Spiral Review ~10 min

Mixed Facts

  • 2×4 = ?
  • 2×5 = ?
  • 10 ÷ 2 = ?
  • Spell and define: harbor

Short Match

Match the word to its meaning (drag-and-drop in interactive build).

  • Constellation → A group of stars forming a pattern
  • Mammal → An animal that breathes air and feeds milk to its young

4) Reading 1 — Fiction ~10–12 min

Adapted Norse legend (early Grade 3 reading level). [Sample excerpt]

On a misty morning, Leif watched the seabirds circle. “Follow the puffins,” his father said, “and they will lead you to safe shores.” The stars slept above, but their paths were still in Leif’s mind.
Comprehension & Language
  1. Who told Leif to follow the puffins?
  2. What do the birds help the sailors find?
  3. Underline a past-tense verb in the passage.

5) Reading 2 — Nonfiction ~10–12 min

Title: How Norse Sailors Read Nature

  • Birds: Puffins and other seabirds fly toward land to feed and nest.
  • Mammals: Seals often rest on rocks near shorelines.
  • Stars: The North Star helps sailors know which way is north.
Check Your Understanding
  1. Why would following puffins help a sailor?
  2. Which star helps find direction at night?
  3. Circle: A seal on a rock means you are likely (far from shore / near shore).

6) Applied Activity — Data Table ~15–20 min

Record the number and types of fish a Norwegian fisherman catches each day. Then answer the questions.

DayCodHerringMackerel
Mon462
Tue354

Fri631

Questions

  1. On which day did the fisherman catch the most cod?
  2. How many total herring were caught on Wed and Thu?
  3. Which fish was caught the least on Fri?
  4. Make a bar sketch of cod catches across the week.

7) Wrap-Up ~5 min

  1. Exit Ticket: (a) 2×4 = ? (b) Name one animal Norse sailors watched to find land. (c) Which star points north?

Preview of Tomorrow: We’ll learn how the Big Dipper points to the North Star.

8) Discovery & Enrichment (Optional)

Discovery Learning

  • Use a night-sky app to locate the North Star and Big Dipper.
  • Mini-research: Find two seabirds that nest on cliffs in Norway.
  • Create a simple compass using a magnetized needle and water.

Classroom Fun

  • Role-play: “Ship crew” reads bird and star clues to choose a route.
  • Group challenge: Build a star map poster with labels.

Cross‑Media Picks

  • Ticket to Ride: Nordic Countries (board game) — geography practice.
  • National Geographic Kids: Night Sky (book).
  • Short documentary clip: Arctic seabirds (teacher‑vetted).
